Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 June 2026

This policy describes what personal data the recommend app (a personalised movie-recommendation web app) processes, for what purpose and on what basis, and the rights you have in relation to that processing.

1. Data controller

The data controller is Paweł Krupiński, the creator of the recommend app. For any privacy or data-protection matter you can reach us at pawel.krupinski@gmail.com.

2. What data we process

After you sign in we process:

  • Login data from Facebook or Google — your user id at the provider, your email address, your display name, and your profile picture URL.
  • Your activity in the app — the movie ratings you record or import, titles you dismiss or mark as "not seen", and your preferences (country and selected streaming services), synced across your devices.
  • An essential cookie — a signed session cookie that keeps you logged in. We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising.

Movie metadata, ratings and artwork shown in the app come from public third-party sources (e.g. TMDB) and are not personal data of our users.

3. Purpose and legal basis

  • Authenticating you and running your account, and tailoring recommendations to your taste — necessary to provide the service you asked for (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR).
  • Keeping the app secure and working correctly — our legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

4. Who we share data with

We do not sell your personal data and do not share it with third parties for marketing. Data is only processed by the providers that technically enable the app to run, acting on our behalf:

  • Login providers — Meta (Facebook) and Google, from whom the login data originates, under their own privacy policies.
  • Infrastructure providers — the hosting platform and the database where your account is stored.

5. How long we keep your data

We keep your account data for as long as you use the app. When your account is deleted, all data associated with it is permanently erased (see section 7).

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • access your data and obtain a copy of it,
  • rectify (correct) your data,
  • erase your data ("right to be forgotten"),
  • restrict processing,
  • data portability,
  • object to processing,
  • lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (in Poland, the President of the Personal Data Protection Office, PUODO).

7. Deleting your data

You can delete your account together with all of its data at any time directly in the app — open Settings and choose Delete account. This permanently erases your profile, ratings and saved preferences.

If you signed in with Facebook, you can also remove the app from your Facebook settings and request deletion there; Facebook will notify us through its data-deletion callback and we will delete all data linked to your Facebook login. You can confirm deletion at our data-deletion status page. Alternatively, request deletion any time by emailing pawel.krupinski@gmail.com.

8. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the app evolves. The current version is always available at this address, with the date of the last update shown at the top of the page.
